# Product Definition

## What This Product Is

This app is a Supervisor Execution System.

Its primary job is not to help managers discover who is struggling.
Its primary job is to help managers follow through consistently, track outcomes, and build discipline over time.

The product answers three questions:

1. What needs my attention right now?
2. What should I do next?
3. Did it work?

## What This Product Is Not

This app is not primarily:

- a dashboard for passive monitoring
- an analytics product focused on more visibility
- a discovery tool for identifying obvious low performers
- a reporting system used only when leadership asks for a summary

Dashboards, rankings, and trends still matter, but they are supporting inputs.
They are not the main product experience.

## Core Language

Prefer this language in planning, design, and feature decisions:

- action queue
- unresolved issue
- follow-up due
- repeat issue
- outcome tracking
- supervisor discipline
- follow-through

Avoid centering the product around this language:

- dashboard
- low performers
- below goal
- insights
- analytics

## Product Thesis

Managers already know who is struggling.

The product advantage is not superior discovery.
The advantage is helping supervisors:

- act consistently
- avoid dropping follow-ups
- remember what happened last time
- justify decisions with evidence
- avoid wasting time on the wrong people

## Today Screen

The default screen is Today.

The Today Screen is for:

- open actions
- overdue follow-ups
- unresolved issues with no action taken
- repeated no-improvement cycles
- high performers with no development follow-through

The Today Screen is not for showcasing charts first.
If a chart or ranking appears, it exists to support an action decision.

## Main Object: Action

The main object in the product is now an Action.

An Action represents a supervisor-owned follow-through cycle around a person or issue.

An Action should answer:

- why this needs attention
- what should happen next
- when follow-up is due
- what success looks like
- what actually happened

Rankings, trends, flags, and coaching notes feed into Actions.
They are inputs, not the center of the experience.

## Inputs vs Outputs

Inputs:

- UPH history
- employee records
- submissions/imported work data
- rankings
- benchmark comparisons
- trends
- coaching notes

Outputs:

- action queue
- overdue follow-up list
- repeat issue detection
- outcome history
- supervisor accountability and evidence

## Success Definition

The product is successful when supervisors use it to improve follow-through, not just visibility.

Key signs of success:

- fewer unresolved issues with no owner
- fewer overdue follow-ups
- clearer action histories per employee
- faster time from issue detection to action taken
- measurable outcome tracking after coaching or intervention

## Product Direction Guardrail

When deciding what to build next, prefer features that improve follow-through.

Good:

- action lifecycle
- follow-up scheduling
- repeat issue detection
- outcome capture
- evidence for prior decisions

Low priority unless they clearly support action:

- more charts
- more dashboards
- more passive analytics panels

## Current Transition Rule

During the transition, existing ranking, risk, and trend systems stay in place.

They should be treated as the signal engine.
They trigger and support Actions.
They should not dominate the UI hierarchy.
